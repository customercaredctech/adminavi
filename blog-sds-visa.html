How to Secure a Canadian Visa Under the Student Direct Stream (SDS)

June 28, 2026 By Student Advisory Panel 5 Min Read

For international students applying to study in Canada from countries like India, Pakistan, Nigeria, and the Philippines, the Student Direct Stream (SDS) is the fastest pathway to visa approvals. SDS applications are typically processed within 20 calendar days compared to months for the regular non-SDS stream.

However, meeting SDS criteria requires precise compliance. A single missing document will automatically demote your application to the non-SDS stream, significantly delaying your travel plans.

Core SDS Criteria Checklists

To file your visa application under the SDS stream, you must submit proof of the following elements:

  • Language Proficiency Score: An overall IELTS Academic score of 6.0 or higher (or equivalent TOEFL / PTE Academic scores) written within the past two years.
  • Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC): A GIC of $20,635 CAD from an eligible Canadian financial institution (e.g. Scotiabank, ICICI Bank, CIBC) representing proof of living costs.
  • First Year Tuition Payment: Receipt showing full payment of tuition for your first year of studies at your DLI.
  • Letter of Acceptance: An official LOA from a Designated Learning Institution in Canada.
  • Medical Exam and Police Certificates: Front-up medical clearance sheets from paneled physicians.
"Double-check your IELTS scores. Under SDS, even if your overall score is 6.5, if a single sub-band (e.g. Writing) falls below 6.0, IRCC policy may defer your profile to regular processing, raising your denial risk." — IRCC Advisory Panel

Filing Tips for Success

Make sure your study plan (Statement of Purpose - SOP) explains exactly why you chose this specific course, why you chose Canada, and your plans to return to your home country upon graduation to establish strong ties.
