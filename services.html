What We Offer

Complete Admission Advisory Services

From career counselling to pre-departure briefings — we support you at every single step of your Canadian education journey.

1
Counselling

Educational Counselling

Our certified education counselors conduct structured, in-depth one-on-one sessions to fully understand your academic history, test score status, career aspirations, provincial preferences, and overall budget before recommending any course of action.

We do not provide a generic list of colleges. Every recommendation is based on a detailed academic profiling exercise designed specifically for your unique situation.

  • In-depth academic background review (10th, 12th, Graduation transcripts)
  • IELTS/PTE/TOEFL score assessment and gap analysis
  • Long-term career roadmap planning aligned with Canadian labour market
  • Budget and funding feasibility evaluation
2
Institution Selection

College & University Selection

With direct partnerships at over 150 Designated Learning Institutions (DLIs) across Canada, we shortlist the most suitable institutions for your profile. We consider academic requirements, tuition fees, intake availability, PGWP eligibility, and campus location.

We distinguish between public colleges (typically offering lower tuition and higher PGWP eligibility) and research-intensive universities, helping you choose the best pathway for your financial and career goals.

  • Shortlisting 3–5 best-matched DLIs across Ontario, BC, Alberta, and Atlantic Canada
  • Analysis of PGWP duration eligibility per institution
  • On-campus vs. off-campus accommodation availability
  • Intake timeline matching (September, January, May)
3
Program Selection

Program Selection

Choosing the right program code is critical — not just for admission, but for your subsequent study permit and PGWP application. We map trending, high-demand programs in fields like STEM, Business, Health Sciences, and Hospitality that match Canadian labour market projections.

  • Diploma, Advanced Diploma, Post-Graduate Certificate, Bachelor's & Master's programs
  • Co-op and internship-integrated programs for early work experience
  • Programs with strong NOC code alignment for future PR pathways
  • Credit transfer options from your existing degree
4
Admission

Admission Guidance

Our dedicated admissions officers manage the entire application process on your behalf. We prepare, review, and compile all required academic documents, language test scores, reference letters, and supporting materials — then submit them directly to the college representatives we work with.

  • SOP (Statement of Purpose) writing, editing, and review
  • Transcript formatting, notarization, and back-log certificate guidance
  • Letter of Recommendation (LOR) drafting assistance
  • Direct application submission through our college partnerships for faster LOA turnaround
  • Tuition deposit and initial enrollment fee payment guidance
5
Scholarships

Scholarship Guidance

Canadian colleges offer a wide range of entrance scholarships, merit bursaries, and regional grants to international students that are often under-utilized. We identify eligibility windows and guide you through the application process to reduce your financial burden significantly.

  • Entrance scholarship identification per institution
  • Scholarship application essay review and optimization
  • Government of Canada bursary and grants information
  • External organization scholarship databases
6
Optional

Interview Preparation (Optional)

Some elite master's programs, health sciences programs, and selective colleges require an admission interview as part of the selection process. Our experienced coaches run targeted mock interviews, provide feedback on tone and structure, and help you respond confidently.

  • Mock admission interview sessions (video/phone format)
  • Common question bank preparation for your specific program
  • Body language, clarity of speech, and professional presentation coaching
  • Verbal explanation of your academic gaps or background if needed
7
Pre-Arrival

Pre-Arrival Guidance

Your journey doesn't end with a study permit. We prepare you completely for landing in Canada, from opening your GIC-linked bank account, to understanding provincial health insurance, what to carry at the border, and finding your first accommodation.

  • GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) account setup: Scotiabank/CIBC guidance
  • Provincial health card enrollment (OHIP, MSP, AHCIP)
  • CBSA port of entry documents checklist
  • SIN (Social Insurance Number) registration on arrival
  • Temporary and permanent housing search strategies
  • Weather, culture, and campus orientation briefing

Service FAQs

Are your services free of charge?
The initial profile assessment and counselling consultation is completely free. Service fees for full application management are discussed transparently at the time of engagement.
Do you guarantee visa approval?
No. We provide educational consulting services and help you build the strongest possible application, but final visa decisions are made solely by IRCC. We do not provide immigration legal advice.
How long does the full process take from consultation to visa?
Typically 3–5 months: 2–4 weeks for offer letter, 4–6 weeks for study permit under SDS. We plan your timeline based on the target intake date.
