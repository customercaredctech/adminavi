Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common queries regarding Canadian colleges and student visas.

What is the SDS program for Canadian Visas?
The Student Direct Stream (SDS) is an expedited study permit processing program for students from certain countries. It requires upfront proof of tuition payment, a GIC of $20,635 CAD, and valid language test scores.
How much does it cost to study in Canada?
Tuition fees vary significantly by program and institution, typically ranging from $15,000 to $35,000 CAD per year. You also need to demonstrate living expenses of approximately $20,635 CAD for your first year.
Do you charge for the initial consultation?
No, our initial profile assessment and consultation is completely free of charge.
Can I work in Canada after I graduate?
Yes, if you graduate from a designated learning institution (DLI) with an eligible program, you can apply for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) valid for up to 3 years.
